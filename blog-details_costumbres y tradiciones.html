COSTUMBRES Y TRADICIONES

QAMILI

The Qamili is a dance of the fertility of the earth, because it is only danced in the sowing and in all its execution ritual. This fact coincided with the enormous transcendence that this period assumes in the agricultural cycle of the Andean communities and that in this particular one it is consecrated ritually in offering of the land, seeds and plows. Thus, the sowing of the platforms of the saints marks the beginning of those activities for the group of communal farmers being the object of a ritual use whose central axis is the dance of the Qamili; without excluding the patron saint, the Qamili is identified with the image of San Isidro Labrador, whose association to the agricultural works is evident. It is fundamental to point out a feature of the Qamili, it is not a dance that represents the sowing, but it is the sowing itself executed by groups of dancers to the rhythm of the music. The Ccolla is in charge of throwing the seed to the right place accompanying the Ga√±an San Isidro. After them, the qamiles (men) and the Collas (women) sow with the characteristic tool of the place called Chaquitaclla in the small platforms where the bulls can not enter. Finally, the group of men who are dedicated to fixing broken platforms is called Ccanitas. With all these characters, the dance of the Ccamile is born, which seeks to represent an everyday but significant event.

WITI

Dance declared intangible cultural heritage of humanity. The dance of the Wititi of the Maca district is a traditional popular dance related to the beginning of adulthood. It takes the form of a ritual of love courtship and is usually performed by young people during the religious festivities that are held throughout the rainy season, where the most important is held on February 2 each year in honor of the Virgin of Candelaria. The couples of dancers line up in rows and perform various steps to the rhythm of the music. The dancers wear finely embroidered costumes with colorful natural motifs and are played with characteristic hats. For their part, the dancers wear two superimposed women's skirts, a military shirt, a shawl and hats with attachments. The representation of the wititi coincides with the beginning of the agricultural production cycle and symbolizes the renewal of nature and society.

MALLKY PALLAY

Represents weavers of the pre-Hispanic era, who idea the pallares (iconography of weaving). It is a dance that tries to stage the weaving using multicolored ribbons that give it the similar characteristic of the Rainbow. The dress that is worn is like a steel maga polo of blue color, a belt called chumpi, it also wears a kind of skirt above the knees in which you can see the color yellow, red, green. He wears a headdress, ornaments on his arms and knees, and also wears flip-flops. Other participants in the dance, are the soldier and his companion, the soldier is responsible for grabbing and carrying the mallki (stick) while his companion is a small person dressed as a monkey who supports the soldier
